Effects on life


Overall Effects of Drugs:

Drugs may seem enjoyable at first, but their long-term effects are dangerous and often life-destroying. Here are the main harms caused by drug use:




Damage to the Brain:

Drugs affect your thinking, memory, and emotions. They can cause anxiety, depression, confusion, and even lead to mental illness.




Harm to the Body:

Drug use can badly damage important organs like the liver, lungs, heart, and kidneys, leading to serious health problems or even.




Addiction:

Once you start using drugs, your body may become dependent. You lose control and feel the constant need to use them again and again.




Broken Relationships:

Drugs often ruin family bonds and friendships. The person becomes distant, angry, or dishonest, leading to trust issues and loneliness.




Loss of Education or Job:

Many drug users drop out of school or lose their jobs because they can’t concentrate or perform well.




Crime and Legal Trouble:

Drug addiction can push people into crime, such as stealing or selling drugs, which leads to arrest, fines, or jail.




Wasted Life:

In the end, drugs destroy a person’s future, health, time, and happiness. Instead of success, they bring regret and pain.
